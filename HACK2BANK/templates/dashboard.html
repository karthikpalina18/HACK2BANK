🚨Insider Data Leaks in Banks

📌A recent report reveals that low-paid bank employees have been selling client data to online scammers,
exposing a critical security vulnerability within financial institutions. Despite previous warnings, 
insider data leaks have surged, leading to an increase in elder fraud complaints. For instance, 
a Toronto-Dominion Bank employee leaked customer information via Telegram, and a Navy Federal 
Credit Union staffer sold customer data.
                

                    
🚨 Bank Fraud Cases Surge in India

📌 The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) reports a significant increase in bank frauds during the first half 
of the fiscal year 2024-25. Between April and September, 18,461 fraud cases were reported, involving a
 total of ₹21,367 crore. This marks a substantial rise compared to the same period in the previous year,
which saw 14,480 cases amounting to ₹2,623 crore.
                

                    
🚨 Bank Manager Arrested for Data Theft and Fraud

📌 In December 2024, a bank manager was arrested for allegedly stealing data and defrauding a company of
 ₹12.51 crore. The manager, along with accomplices, accessed confidential customer information to execute
  unauthorized transactions, highlighting serious lapses in internal security measures within the bank.        
                

                    
🚨 ICICI Bank-Videocon Loan Fraud Case

📌 The ICICI Bank-Videocon loan scam involves allegations against former ICICI Bank CEO Chanda Kochhar,
  who is accused of sanctioning loans to Videocon Group companies in exchange for personal benefits.
  Investigations revealed that between 2009 and 2011, ICICI Bank sanctioned six high-value loans to
  Videocon Group, which later became non-performing assets, causing significant financial losses.
  In December 2022, Chanda Kochhar, her husband Deepak Kochhar, and Videocon's Venugopal Dhoot were
  arrested in connection with the case.
